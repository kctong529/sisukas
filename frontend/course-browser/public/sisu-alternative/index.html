What SISU does well, and where it falls short

SISU is the official study information system used by Finnish universities. It works well for course registration, degree requirements, and official records.

The problem is not that SISU is broken. The problem is that SISU is built for administration, not exploration. Planning rarely starts with “I already know exactly what to register for”.

Why students look for a SISU alternative

When planning studies, students often want to:

  • explore multiple course options side by side
  • compare schedules across periods
  • understand overlaps and trade-offs
  • filter courses quickly using constraints that actually matter

In SISU, these tasks tend to require many clicks, tab switching, and a lot of mental bookkeeping. That makes reasoning about choices harder than it needs to be.

How Sisukas complements SISU

Sisukas does not replace SISU. It focuses on the phase before registration: exploration, comparison, and decision-making.

Sisukas helps you discover relevant courses fast, apply clear filters, and see schedule conflicts explicitly. Once you decide, SISU remains the source of truth for registration.

Sisukas is intentionally narrow: it optimises for thinking, not administration.

Who Sisukas is for

Sisukas is especially useful for:

  • students planning multiple alternatives
  • students balancing overlapping courses
  • students who want to understand schedules before committing
  • students who find SISU slow or cognitively heavy for planning
